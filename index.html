Real Engagement,
Not Just a Rollout

Any new strategy will fail if it’s just a memo sent from the top floor. We must move from "Installation" to true "Engagement."

The Trap of "Installation"

Announcing a "Premium Plan" via email. Top-down mandates. Ignoring staff reality.

The Power of "Engagement"

Involving front-line staff in Discovery. Giving them a choice. Building belief.

Front-Line Discovery

Involve technicians and call center staff. They know what a "premium experience" actually looks like because they hear the complaints every day.

Addressing Resistance

The sales team is terrified of losing customers without discounts. We must address this fear directly and prove the sustainability of high-margin plans.

Action Meetings

Replace "rollout memos" with internal Action Meetings. Give staff the power of choice in how they deliver this new value.

Technology with a Human Touch

Silknet wins on mass bundles, but they feel like a faceless utility. This is our opening.

The Myth of Convenience

Technology is great for efficiency, but it sucks at building trust. Automated loops ("Press 1 for Sales") alienate high-value clients.

The "Average" Market

In a market that feels increasingly transactional and average, pure efficiency is no longer a differentiator.

Human-Centered Service

Imagine a customer calling in and actually reaching a person who knows their name. That presence is our powerful differentiator.
